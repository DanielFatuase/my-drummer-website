About Me

The boy who couldn't
stop breaking pencils.

I'm Daniel Ayo, a drummer with a journey that started long before I ever sat behind a proper drum set. Some of my earliest memories take me back to when I was just four or five years old — turning pencils and rulers into drumsticks, drumming on every surface within reach.

What began as childhood curiosity became passion. Passion became skill. Skill became service. Today, I bring that same relentless energy to every stage, studio, and lesson — from church worship teams to live performances and recording sessions.

"I naturally gravitated toward a more refined and contemporary approach. Different as my style was, it became an important part of my musical identity."
My Journey

The Story Behind
the Sticks

The Beginning

While other children spent their free time playing games, I spent mine turning pencils and rulers into drumsticks. Every day after school, I would drum on anything I could find — the window pavement, the staircase, the walls, tables, and virtually every surface within reach. The result was always the same: broken pencils, broken rulers, and endless complaints from my siblings and my mother. At the time, I had only one concern: finding the next surface to create a rhythm on.

One Groove

At church, I became fascinated by music through my elder brothers, who were actively involved in the church music department. They eventually allowed me to play the drums during midweek services. The funny thing was that I only knew one groove. Just one. No matter what song was being played, somehow that same groove found its way into everything. My brothers laughed about it often, and honestly, they had every right to. But that single groove was the foundation of everything that would come later.

Marching Band Days

I developed a deep admiration for the senior students in my primary school's marching band. As a Primary Three pupil, I would watch them perform with excitement and wonder. By Primary Four, I had become friendly with some of them and occasionally got opportunities to play. By Primary Five, my friend Samuel and I had become key members of the band. We played with energy, enthusiasm, and absolutely no fear.

Boarding School & Pelumi

A transfer to boarding school surrounded me with a community of drummers and musicians. There, I met Pelumi, who introduced me to a more structured approach to drumming. He taught me rudiments, technique, and discipline. We practiced together constantly, sharpening our skills and growing as musicians. By Senior Secondary Three, we had become the leading drummers in the school.

The University Turning Point

Shortly after joining a church near my university, I attended a service and found myself paying close attention to the music team. Confident that I could contribute, I approached the pastor and offered to serve as a drummer. The following week, I was given the opportunity. I played. People noticed. And from that moment, everything changed.

Still Being Written

I became actively involved in the church's teenage ministry, eventually rising to lead the choir and music department. I began receiving opportunities to play for the main church choir, then larger settings, bigger responsibilities, and continuous improvement as a musician. The journey has continued through churches, events, collaborations, performances, and countless opportunities to learn, serve, and grow.

What began as childhood curiosity became passion. Passion became skill. Skill became service. And the story is still being written.
